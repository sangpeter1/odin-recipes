Chicken Curry

This is a classic chicke curry that uses a variety of a blend of whole and ground spices. This is another great recipe for the meal prep rotation. Also, chicken could be easily substituted to make an equally delicious dahl curry.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoon of cranola oil (or) butter (or) ghee
  • 1 stick of cinamon
  • 1 or 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of whole cumins
  • 2-3 pieces of cloves
  • 2 pods of cardamons
  • 1 teaspoon of red chile powder
  • 1 teasppon of tumeric
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 read onion chopped
  • 3 or 4 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 inch of tumeric minced or grounded
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
    • 4-5 medium tomatoes chopped
  • 1 lb of boneless chicken thigh
  • 1 batch of cilantro
Steps
  1. Add oil into a pan or a wok and cook the whole spices in medium heat (cinanom, bay leaves, cumin, cloves, cardamons) for about one minute or as soon as the cumin seeds start to pop.
  2. Add the onions and cook until they become translucent. Then add the garlic and ginger until the raw smell goes away.
  3. Add the dry spices (tumeric, chile powder, cumin powder, ground coriander) and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes. Reduce the heat to medium-low. Cover the tomatoes until they're completely broken down -- for about 15 minutes.
  5. While the tomates are cooking, chop up the chicken thighs into 1-inch cubes. Add the chicken into the curry paste once the tomates are borken down. Add a cup of water and cover the pan or wok. Cook for another 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked
  6. Add salt and the garam masala. Cook for another 3-5 minutes.
  7. Chop up the cilntro and add it to the curry at the end. Stir until it all mixes up into the curry.
  8. Serve with white or brown rice.
